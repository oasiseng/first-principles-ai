# Framework Crosswalk

| Principle | EU AI Act | NIST AI RMF | ISO/IEC 42001 | Anthropic / OpenAI / Google |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Human authority is non-negotiable | Art. 14 (human oversight) | GOVERN | Accountability and oversight controls | Anthropic safe-first priority; OpenAI human control red-lines |
| Safety scales with capability | GPAI/systemic risk obligations | MAP + MANAGE risk tiers | Risk treatment by context | Anthropic ASL; OpenAI Preparedness thresholds; Google CCL tiers |
| Transparency is the default | Transparency obligations | MAP (context/impact communication) | Transparency and documentation controls | Company model behavior and disclosure requirements |
| Harm has owners | Provider/deployer obligations | GOVERN (roles, accountability) | Organizational accountability and incident ownership | Safety governance ownership patterns |
| Test before you trust | Conformity and post-market monitoring | MEASURE + MANAGE | Monitoring, evaluation, continual improvement | Preparedness evaluations, red-teaming, deployment gates |
| Honesty is structural | Prohibition and risk controls against manipulation/deception | MANAGE trustworthiness outcomes | Integrity and communication controls | Truthfulness/honesty constraints across specs |
| Principles evolve; rights don't | Fundamental rights framework | GOVERN lifecycle governance | Management system review and updates | Public policy updates with versioned releases |

> This crosswalk is a practical alignment aid, not legal advice.
